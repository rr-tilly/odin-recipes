Tiramisu

Description

Tiramisu is a classic Italian dessert known for its layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers, creamy mascarpone filling, and a dusting of cocoa powder. This rich and indulgent treat is the perfect ending to a dinner party or a comforting sweet snack for any occasion.

Its unique combination of flavors and textures makes it a crowd-pleaser, and the no-bake preparation means it's simple to make in advance.

Ingredients

  • 300g ladyfinger biscuits
  • 250g mascarpone cheese
  • 3 large eggs (separated into yolks and whites)
  • 100g granulated sugar
  • 300ml strong brewed coffee (cooled)
  • 2 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder (for dusting)
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, whisk together egg yolks and sugar until pale and creamy.
  2. Fold in the mascarpone cheese until smooth and well combined.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the egg whites until stiff peaks form, then gently fold them into the mascarpone mixture.
  4. Mix the cooled coffee with the coffee liqueur (if using) in a shallow dish.
  5. Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture and layer them in a rectangular dish or individual serving glasses.
  6. Spread a layer of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers.
  7. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with the mascarpone mixture on top.
  8. Dust the top generously with cocoa powder and garnish with chocolate shavings if desired.
  9. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours (preferably overnight) before serving.
